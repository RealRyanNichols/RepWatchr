# Court And Prosecutor Profile Policy

RepWatchr may profile judges, prosecutors, district attorneys, court officials, public courts, and related public power roles when the profile stays within public-role records and source-backed civic accountability.

## Public Boundary

Every court/prosecutor profile must show the shared public-role boundary:

> This profile covers public-role records only. RepWatchr does not publish private home addresses, minor children, or harassment instructions. Submit corrections or public sources below.

## Allowed Profile Scope

- Official court page, prosecutor office page, public role page, or agency directory.
- Public docket link or public filing where lawful.
- Public budget, public meeting minutes, public appointment/election record, or public statement.
- Official press release.
- Public records response.
- Campaign finance only if the role is elected and the record is a public filing.
- Public question prompts that ask where a record can be found.

## Extra Safety Rules

Court and prosecutor profiles require restrained copy. They must not look like attack pages, wanted posters, mugshot pages, or harassment pages.

Do not publish:

- Home addresses or private contact details.
- Family, spouse, or minor-child information.
- Private case-party details beyond what is necessary and lawful in a public filing context.
- Sealed, restricted, juvenile, medical, or private financial information.
- Claims that a judge, prosecutor, or court official committed a crime unless an official public legal finding supports that exact statement.
- Calls to confront, target, harass, shame, or threaten a person.

## Source Labels

Court/prosecutor records should use one of:

- Confirmed public record
- Source-backed public record
- Public question
- Under review
- Needs source

When in doubt, use "Under review" or "Public question."

## Incident, Docket, And Case Mentions

Public case references can appear only when:

- The source is an official public docket, court filing, official agency page, official public statement, or lawful public records response.
- The summary is narrow and cautious.
- The item explains what the source does not prove when needed.
- Admin review has approved the item for public display.

RepWatchr should not infer guilt, bias, misconduct, or corruption from a case link by itself.

## Safe Public Questions

Preferred question format:

- Where is the official public docket for this matter?
- Which public source confirms the current officeholder?
- Where can residents find the public information request page?
- Where can the public view meeting minutes, budget records, or appointment records?

## Admin Review

Admin edits should trigger warnings for:

- Criminal accusation language
- Guilt language
- Personal address patterns
- Family/minor terms
- Threats
- Harassment wording
- Unsupported allegation wording

The admin must pick a public-role label before staging profile edits.

## Analytics

The module tracks:

- `court_profile_open`
- `public_role_boundary_viewed`
- `public_info_source_clicked`
- `badge_profile_correction_clicked`
- `badge_profile_source_submitted`
- `safety_report_submitted`

The analytics are for source workflow, product improvement, and auditability.
