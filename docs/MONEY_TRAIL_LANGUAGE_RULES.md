# Money Trail Language Rules

RepWatchr money pages must stay neutral, source-backed, and defensible.

## Required Rule

Use this public explanation anywhere donor or spending records are shown:

> Campaign finance records show public filings. RepWatchr does not imply wrongdoing from a contribution or expenditure by itself.

## Safe Phrases

- Public campaign finance filing
- Reported contribution
- Reported expenditure
- Donor/source aggregate
- Committee filing source
- Public vendor record
- Public procurement record
- Needs filing source
- Needs committee match
- Source-backed public record
- Aggregate filing summary

## Unsafe Phrases Unless Legally Established and Sourced

- Bought
- Bribed
- Corrupt
- Payoff
- Owned by
- Controlled by
- Took dirty money
- Criminal donor
- Kickback
- Illegal contribution

## How To Explain Relevance

Use:

- “Voters can inspect whether donor patterns align with public votes, committee work, contracts, or policy priorities.”
- “This record is useful context, not proof of influence.”
- “Open the filing before making a stronger claim.”
- “This row needs itemized source review.”

Avoid:

- “This proves the official voted for the donor.”
- “This donor controls the official.”
- “This money is corrupt.”

## Donor And Vendor Privacy Boundary

Use public campaign finance and public procurement/vendor records only.

Do not publish:

- private financial information
- bank information
- private home addresses
- family/minor details
- personal contact information not required by the public filing display

Donor/PAC pages should stay `noindex` unless they have clear public value, source quality, and privacy review.
