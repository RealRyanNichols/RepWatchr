# 2026 Midterm Launch Plan

Status: working editorial plan  
Signal review date: July 15, 2026

## Recommended coverage shape

RepWatchr should launch with Texas depth and a bounded national watchlist. This is more credible than attempting thin profiles for every race and more discoverable than covering only East Texas.

| Option | Advantage | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| East Texas only | Distinctive local expertise and manageable sourcing | Limited discovery and few nationally competitive races |
| All Texas | Strong statewide brand and rich election records | Large profile and filing-review workload |
| National battlegrounds | High search interest | Easy to become shallow or horse-race driven |
| Texas depth + national watchlist | Local authority with national reach | Requires strict research tiers |

The recommended first cohort is:

1. Gold-standard dossiers for Texas U.S. Senate, governor, U.S. House districts 1, 4, and 5, and East Texas state Senate districts 1–5.
2. A statewide directory for every Texas federal and statewide general-election nominee with verified identity, ballot status, filing source, official links, and visible research gaps.
3. A national watchlist for Senate races in Georgia, Michigan, North Carolina, Maine, Ohio, Alaska, and Texas, followed by eight to ten House-control races.
4. Candidate lists remain provisional until filing, withdrawal, replacement, and ballot-order windows are closed and sourced. Texas write-in filing runs July 18 through August 17, 2026. See the [Texas Secretary of State candidate guide](https://www.sos.state.tx.us/elections/candidates/guide/2026/writein2026.shtml).

## Publication and ranking rules

- A story must have `editorialStatus: approved`, `sourceStatus: source_linked`, a reviewer, review time, correction state, topic key, and direct receipts.
- Ranking reads recency, primary-source count, independent-publisher count, midterm relevance, and receipt count.
- Ranking never reads a politician's name, party, ideology, source creator, or paid status.
- In the leading three items, one person or topic may appear only once. The top ten allow no more than two appearances per person/topic before deferred items.
- A public social post proves what that account published. It does not prove the post's factual claims.
- Platform-native embeds are preferred to copied video or screenshots. Store URL, author, retrieval/review time, context, and source status.
- Social distribution remains human-approved. Trend ingestion cannot publish or autopost by itself.

## Balanced capacity target

- 35% household economics
- 25% accountability receipts
- 15% election structure
- 10% immigration and national security
- 10% AI, deepfakes, data centers, and technology policy
- 5% abortion and other high-conflict ballot/cultural issues

Balance means the same questions, source hierarchy, response window, correction process, and completeness rule for every candidate—not artificial equal weight for every topic.
